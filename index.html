Interactive Energy and Air Pollution Study

Click/Drag/Hover to Interact with the Charts Below

Since 2000, Coal-based energy production has dropped by over 20%. The 2011 EPA rules regulating coal-based pollution emissions (under the Mercury and Air Toxics Standards (MATS)) under the Obama Administration coincides with the particularly sharp drop-off in coal-based energy production around that time.

Over that same time period, combined emissions of three of the most harmful air pollutants to respitory health (that coal-based energy production contributes to), namely CO, NOx, and SO2, have declined by over 45%.

The bar chart to the right shows the distribution of the cost of electricity by state in 2000.

Now, the bar chart to the right shows the distribution of the cost of electricity by state in 2017. The shape of the distribution is similar between years. Adjusted for inflation, electricity costs nationally have risen just 2.9% -- despite the clear shift away from coal-based energy production in that time.

A Pearson Correlation Coefficient calculation of the correlation between average cost of electricity and average air quality index = 0.286. As such, these two variables are moderately positively correlated.
