Coal-based energy production has dropped substantially
since 2000.

The 2011 adoption of the Mercury and Air Toxics
Standards (MATS) by the Obama Administration's EPA,
which regulated emissions of major coal-based pollutants
like mercury, coincided with the particularly sharp drop-off
in coal energy production.


































Over the same time period, there has also been a marked
decrease in the emissions of three of the most dangerous
air pollutants to respiratory health: CO, NOx, and SO2.
